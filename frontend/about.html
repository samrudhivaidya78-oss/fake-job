About SafeJob AI

Built to protect women
from job scams

Employment fraud targeting women is rising. SafeJob AI fights back — using keyword-based AI to identify fake job offers before they cause financial and emotional harm.

The Problem

Job scams are targeting women

Fraudsters post fake job offers with unrealistic salaries, demand registration fees, request personal photos, and exploit desperate job seekers. Women looking for work-from-home roles are disproportionately targeted.

These scams cause financial loss, emotional trauma, and in extreme cases, trafficking. Spotting the signs is hard when you urgently need income — SafeJob AI makes it easier.

70% of job scam victims are women
₹15K+ average financial loss per victim

🚨 Common Red Flags We Detect

  • 💸
    Fake Fees

    Registration fees, training fees, or "security deposits" demanded before you're officially hired.

  • 📸
    Photo Requests

    "Send your photo to confirm your application" is a major red flag for exploitation or trafficking.

  • ⚡
    Urgency Pressure

    "Act now", "Limited seats", "Offer expires today" — pressure tactics to stop you from thinking clearly.

  • 💰
    Unrealistic Promises

    "Easy money", "Earn lakhs from home", "100% Guaranteed income" — signs of a classic employment scam.

  • 👥
    MLM / Pyramid Schemes

    "Recruit others", "Network marketing", "Be your own boss" — hidden multi-level scam structures.

Under The Hood

How SafeJob AI works

A simple but effective pipeline that processes job text through multiple analysis layers.

1

Text Input via Frontend

The user pastes a job offer into the textarea. The JavaScript frontend sends it as a POST request with JSON body { "text": "..." } to the backend.

2

Flask Backend Receives Request

The Python Flask server at POST /analyze receives the JSON body, validates it, and passes the text to the analysis engine.

3

Keyword-Based NLP Analysis

The engine scans the text against 50+ weighted suspicious keyword patterns using regex. Each match adds its weight to the total risk score (0–100).

4

Risk Classification

Scores 0–30 = Low / "genuine". Scores 31–60 = Medium / "fake". Scores 61+ = High / "fake". The response includes risk_score, classification, and flagged_keywords.

5

Stored in Supabase

Every scan is saved to the job_scans table in Supabase with the text, result, score, and timestamp for analysis and tracking scam trends.

6

Results Displayed

The frontend renders the animated SVG risk ring, keyword tags, highlighted text, warning message, and safety tips based on the API response.
